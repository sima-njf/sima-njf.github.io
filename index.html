Sima Khoei

I’m a Biostatistics Ph.D. student at the University of Utah, passionate about creating machine‑learning tools for infectious‑disease modeling and public‑health decision‑making. My work focuses on ML‑based calibration of agent‑based epidemic models—including a three‑layer bidirectional LSTM calibrator being developed as epiworldRcalibrate—and on the comparative evaluation of effective reproduction number (Rt) estimators using agent‑based network models. I collaborate closely with Dr. Bernardo Modenesi, Dr. Yue Zhang, and Dr. George Vega Yon, and contribute to open‑source tools such as epiworldR.

Alongside research, I develop R packages with clear APIs and reproducible workflows. Current efforts include epiworldRcalibrate for practical calibration of epidemic ABMs and imaginarycss for Cognitive Social Structures (software paper in preparation, Sept. 2025). I share my work at national venues including CDC (2024), ENAR (2025), and JSM (2025), aiming to translate rigorous statistics and machine learning into actionable public‑health insight.
